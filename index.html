The Mission

The Green House Foundation preserves historically and culturally significant art, develops artists into sustainable creative careers, and builds the cultural spaces where both endure.

What the Foundation Funds

Six ways we move the mission forward.

01

Cultural Preservation

Cataloging, conservation, and stewardship of significant art collections and cultural artifacts. No limit as to culture, people, or geography.

02

Artist Development

Sponsorships, scholarships, branding, and professional infrastructure that move an artist from passion to profession.

03

Education

Master artist workshops paired with real business training, so creative skill and the ability to earn a living grow together.

04

Music & Healing

Music education, licensed therapy, performances, and instrument lending delivered through galleries, hospitals, schools, and correctional institutions.

05

Community Programming

Exhibitions, cultural events, and an annual pow wow that bring people into the work and keep cultural identity visible and alive.

06

Holistic & Contemplative Arts

Breathwork, meditation, sound healing, movement practice, equine therapy, and plant-medicine education rooted in the arts.

The Immediate Need

A collection that was about to disappear.

A historically significant art collection was at risk of being lost. Today it lives, protected, cared for, and on the walls of the Green House Gallery.

Preserving it is where the Foundation's first dollars go. Some works are irreplaceable. Once they are gone, they are gone for good.

The work can't wait. Preservation is happening now.
