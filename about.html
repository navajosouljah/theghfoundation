About

One valley. Three communities in motion.

The Green House Gallery sits in historic downtown Huntsville, Utah, at the center of a corridor being reshaped by three distinct mountain communities. The Foundation exists so that as this region grows, its cultural identity grows with it.

The Corridor

Read them together.

Three communities, ten minutes apart, are drawing serious attention, investment, and people to this valley. Each one is a natural home for a cultural institution built to last.

Snowbasin Resort
10 minutes away

Ranked #1 in North America by SKI Magazine. Hosted alpine events in the 2002 Winter Olympics. Confirmed alpine venue for the 2034 Salt Lake City Winter Games, bringing global attention to this valley at scale for the first time.

Wasatch Peaks Ranch
Private mountain community

An invitation-only community of roughly 12,700 acres with a dedicated ski resort. People are building permanent homes here for the privacy, the land, and the long-term character. Communities like this eventually seek institutions that reflect who they are.

Powder Mountain
Majority owned by Reed Hastings

Home to Summit Series, a global network of founders, creatives, and social entrepreneurs, and building an outdoor art museum. This community was founded on artistic expression. Its residents are the Foundation's most natural audience.

These three communities are reshaping this valley. The Foundation exists so that as the region grows, its cultural identity grows with it.
